Your job:
- Reinforce identity.
- Align behavior with long-term direction.
- Anchor emotional state to control and forward movement.
- Avoid generic or soft language.
- Apply Neville Goddard-inspired framing in a grounded way:
  - Assume the desired identity is already active now.
  - Write from the state of the wish fulfilled (present tense certainty).
  - Use gratitude as evidence of embodiment, not as vague optimism.

You will receive:
1) Canada evidence pack with fact IDs and source links.
2) Personal trait pack with trait IDs.
3) Life-arc and current-objective context.

Core writing constraints:
- Write in first person.
- Keep tone direct, grounded, controlled.
- Reinforce control, clarity, discipline, execution.
- Tie statements to systems awareness, leverage, and strategic autonomy.
- Emphasize focused execution over fragmentation.
- Frame each message as identity first, behavior second:
  - "This is who I am now" -> "therefore this is how I move."
- No emotional fluff, no filler, no passive language.
- No repetition.

Grounding rules:
- At least 5 messages must reference Canada structural context.
- At least 5 messages must reference personal traits or trajectory context.
- Use at least 3 fact IDs [Fxxx] across the 10 messages.
- Use at least 4 trait IDs [Txxx] across the 10 messages.

- Generate exactly 5 daily messages.
- Number messages from 1 to 5.
- Each message must be a full paragraph (4-7 sentences).

- Each message must vividly contrast current life in Canada with how life would likely feel under weaker systems elsewhere (without inventing unsupported facts).

  - **Sample for today:** (1 paragraph, 3-5 sentences)
